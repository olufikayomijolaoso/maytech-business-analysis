# maytech-business-analysis
Interactive Excel dashboard and business analysis report for Maytech Group, tracking ₦226M+ in profit, regional sales performance, and logistics efficiency.

I recently concluded an analysis of this performance Report, and something immediately jumped out at me. Something absurd that many struggle to explain:
Our operational efficiency is officially declining, yet the company still made a 35.9% profit compared to Last year.

How is that possible?
The standard intuition is that worse operations = less money. But the data told two vastly different stories:
The Bad News (Operations): Our Average Delivery Period is now 7 days. That’s a 15.6% increase in delay compared to the previous year.
The Good News (Finance): Our total profit is ₦226,262,349, an impressive 35.9% increase YoY.
What’s actually going on?
Same period, two opposing trend lines. To get the real answer, I got an insight into the supporting data. What i found was a significant geographical and sales concentration risk.
Geographically: While Lagos is traditionally considered the hub, Port Harcourt is currently generating the absolute highest revenue per city. This confirms that a high-value customer base is highly concentrated in one location.

In Sales: When you look at rep performance, Zainab is absolutely dominating with ₦59.3M, followed by Ibrahim. Together, just two reps are responsible for an overwhelming percentage of the sales volume.
In simpler terms: The massive, high-margin sales from Port Harcourt and a couple of superstar reps are masking major delivery inefficiencies.
Why this matters for the business:
The current success is brittle. This is how high-growth businesses quietly build internal fragility:
We are highly vulnerable to a decline in just one city (Port Harcourt).
Our total revenue depends too heavily on just one or two sales performers.
The delivery delays (7 days) will eventually catch up with us in the form of customer churn, which the massive margins will no longer be able to absorb.

What I professionally advised:
Strengthen Operations in Key Hubs: Immediately fix the delivery pipeline to high-value hubs like Port Harcourt. Do not let logistics break the trust of our best markets.
Duplicate Success (Enablement): Treat Zainab’s process not just as a solo success, but as a framework. We need to formalize her high-volume closing strategy and train other reps like Chioma and Tunde on it immediately to diversify our performer pool.

Operational efficiency is not a luxury; it is the insurance policy for sustained profitability.
